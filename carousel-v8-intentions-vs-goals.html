The Divergence

Two people can pursue
identical goals from
different intentions.

That difference determines what the pursuit costs, what it produces, and whether what gets built compounds or depletes over time.

Same goal. Entirely different experience. Entirely different result.

05
The Illusion

The conscious reason
is usually a rationalization
constructed after the fact.

The actual intention is subconscious — shaped by identity and belief. The conscious mind produces the explanation to make sense of behavior that was already underway.

It sounds like self-awareness. It functions as protection for a subconscious intention the person has never examined.

07
Two Motors
One looks identical
from the outside.
Extrinsic
Proving, protecting
Action powered by what it will get back — approval, safety, avoidance of a feared outcome. Produces results; erodes the operator.
Intrinsic
Expressing, building
Action powered by what it is — the work itself, the value created, who the person becomes doing it. Produces results; compounds the operator.
